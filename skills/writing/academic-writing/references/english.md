# English academic register

Load this when the manuscript is in English.

## Colloquial → objective

- "we found that X" → "the experimental results indicate X" / "the results
  show X" (fields that permit "we" still reserve it for choices the authors
  made — "we adopt", "we restrict" — not for findings).
- "a lot of / a ton of / a bunch of" → "substantial", "numerous", or the
  actual quantity.
- "get / got" → "obtain", "yield"; "look at" → "examine"; "find out" →
  "determine".
- "can't / don't / it's" — no contractions in academic prose.
- Rhetorical questions and direct reader address ("you might wonder") are
  out of register; convert to declarative statements.

## Precision repairs

- Replace vague quantifiers ("significantly better", "much faster") with
  numbers, effect sizes, or confidence intervals. Reserve "significant"
  for statistical significance, stating the test and threshold.
- Hedge only where uncertainty is real, and hedge once: "may suggest" is a
  double hedge; "suggests" or "may indicate" suffices.
- Keep tense conventions: past for what was done and observed, present for
  what the data show and for established facts.
- One term, one concept: once "latency" is chosen, do not alternate with
  "delay" or "lag" for variety — reviewers read the variation as a
  distinction.

## Sentence-shape cautions

- Front-loaded nominal chains ("the utilization of the implementation of")
  hide the verb; prefer verbal constructions ("using", "implementing").
- Long compound sentences chained with "and" split better into cause and
  consequence.
- Passive voice is acceptable where the agent is obvious or irrelevant,
  but methods sections read best when the chosen actor convention (we /
  passive) stays consistent throughout.
